What is our social mission?

Our mission is to help the planet reduce the excess of CO2 in the atmosphere by “Neutralizing the carbon footprint”. We want to create the awareness of taking care of the environment while generating a TRUST place for people to enjoy, invest & be recognized for their contribution.

How does the donation work?

We are partners with a Brazilian area called Reserva Particular do Patrimônio Natural (RPPN) Jacarandás, which has 20 hectares dedicated to reforestation. It is a private-protected area under the Governmental Conservation Program, whose main focus is the preservation of the Atlantic Forest. In addition to helping to regenerate the ecosystem, attract biodiversity and recover the soil, planting helps to foster the local economy, connects participants with the natural environment, and also contributes to the maintenance of the water system around the Cantareira forest. The 5 USD per tree will be used to plant trees including all maintenance over a period of 3 years. Each month, in our community we will share the content of the planted area and all other information.

What does Rarity Ranking Mean?

A rarity score is given to each trait of an NFT based on how many NFTs have that trait. The total rarity score for the NFT is the sum of all the NFT's trait scores. Based on the NFT's overall rarity score, the final rarity rankings will be determined each trait has 4 potential Rarities

  • COMMON Very frequent in the collection (~10-20% of NFTs have this type of items)
  • RARE: Less frequent in the collection (~3-9% of NFTs have this type of items)
  • EPIC: Very weird in the collection (~1-2% of NFTs have this type of items)
  • LEGENDARY: Very exclusive Items (~0.1%-0.5% of NFTs have this type of items)

What Does NFT Mean?

“Non-fungible” means that it is completely unique. “Token” means that it can be transferred on a blockchain. Essentially, NFTs are assets that carry a unique digital identity and can be traded between users on a public blockchain.

Common examples of NFTs include artwork, trading cards, comic books, sports collectibles, games and more. Although NFTs tend to be associated with artwork, they actually represent much more.

How Do NFTs Work?

NFTs can be different things; most often they are represented as artwork, but NFTs can actually unlock a lot of things including digital and in-person experiences, etc.

It works like this - because NFT ownership can be instantly and easily verified on the blockchain, NFTs can act as proof of ownership.

This is helpful in categories like art, where provenance is such an important part of the collectability of a piece.

But this provenance, or proof of ownership is even more useful when applying NFTs to things like experiences; for example, you might in the future use an NFT to unlock access to a digital or in-person gallery or event for a specific artist, with the NFT acting as a ticket or pass to grant you access. The possibilities are really endless.

NFTs empower creators to connect directly with fans and enable new types of exclusive experiences that can be virtual, in-person, or both.

How are NFTs Used?

NFTs can be traded on online marketplaces or exchanged directly between individuals.

NFTs provide a new way for fans and collectors to support the artists they love or as in our case, contribute to reducing the carbon footprint . Since the ownership of NFTs is verifiable on the blockchain, they offer a whole new world of possibilities for engaging between creatives, brands and fans.
